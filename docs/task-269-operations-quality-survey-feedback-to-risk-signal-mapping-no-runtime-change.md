# Task 269 - Operations / Quality Survey Feedback to Risk Signal Mapping / No Runtime Change

## Purpose And Scope

This document extends Task266 through Task268 with docs-only mapping guidance from future customer satisfaction survey feedback to Operations / Quality risk signals.

It defines boundaries between survey score, survey free-text feedback, negative feedback, AI summary, AI risk flag, human review, complaint candidate, callback candidate, quality review candidate, and human-confirmed complaint / callback decisions.

Task269 is documentation-only.

This task is not:

- survey runtime,

- quality review runtime,
- SLA runtime,
- AI risk runtime,

Task269 does not add tables, migrations, schema, indexes, APIs, Admin UI, runtime logic, audit runtime, permission runtime, entitlement runtime, usage runtime, tests, smoke fixtures, package changes, provider sending, LINE / SMS / Email / APP sending, or inventory documentation changes.

## Why Survey Feedback Mapping Is Needed After Task268

Task268 defined complaint / escalation / callback boundaries.

Task269 focuses on the intake side: how future survey feedback may become internal risk signals without becoming automatic official actions.

Survey feedback can be useful, but it is risky if misused.

The platform must avoid:

- treating low score as formal complaint,
- treating negative free text as Case reopened,
- treating AI risk flag as escalation decision,
- treating pricing complaint as settlement approval change,
- treating repeated visit dissatisfaction as a new Field Service Report,
- treating no response as no problem,
- treating positive response as automatic quality closure.

Survey feedback should become structured review context. It should not directly mutate official records.

## Definitions

### Survey Score

Survey score is a customer-submitted rating from a future satisfaction survey.

It is a feedback signal, not official proof of service success or failure by itself.

### Survey Free-text Feedback

Survey free-text feedback is customer-submitted text.

It may contain satisfaction, dissatisfaction, complaint-like wording, unresolved issue details, pricing concern, or safety concern.

It must not be exposed internally or externally without appropriate visibility policy, masking, and review.

### Negative Feedback

Negative feedback is a dissatisfaction signal derived from score, text, or human review.

It is not automatically formal complaint, service failure, Case reopen, Appointment reopen, or report failure.

### Low Score

Low score is a rating below a future product-defined threshold.

Low score may create review need. It must not automatically mutate official status.

### Complaint Candidate

Complaint candidate is a future internal review candidate that may become formal complaint only after human confirmation or future explicitly approved policy.

It is not a formal complaint by default.

### Callback Candidate

Callback candidate is a future internal review candidate suggesting that customer follow-up may be needed.

It is not a callback task or completed callback by default.

### Quality Review Candidate

Quality review candidate is a future internal review candidate suggesting supervisor or quality team should inspect context.

It is not supervisor decision, engineer fault, or official service failure by default.

### AI Summary

AI summary is an advisory summary of customer feedback or related service context.

It is not official customer statement, official complaint, or official review outcome.

### AI Risk Flag

AI risk flag is an advisory signal generated by future AI.

It may help prioritize human review, but it cannot decide complaint, callback, escalation, service recovery, Case status, Appointment status, or Field Service Report status.

### Human-confirmed Complaint

Human-confirmed complaint is a future formal complaint state or record created by authorized human review or explicitly approved deterministic workflow.

Task269 does not implement such record or workflow.

### Human-confirmed Callback Required

Human-confirmed callback required is a future state or record created by authorized human review.

Task269 does not implement callback runtime.

## Mapping Principles

### Survey Score Is Not Formal Complaint

A score can indicate satisfaction level. It cannot by itself create a formal complaint, close a complaint, reopen a Case, or mark a service report failed.

### Free-text Negative Feedback Is Not Case Reopened

Customer text may be serious, but it is still input to review.

Reopening a Case, if ever supported, requires separate explicit workflow, permission, audit, and policy.

### AI Risk Flag Is Not Complaint / Callback / Escalation Decision

AI can flag risk. AI cannot create official complaint, assign callback as completed, close escalation, or decide customer issue is resolved.

### Low Score Can Become Review Signal Only

Low score may become complaint candidate, callback candidate, quality review candidate, or SLA / operations risk signal.

It must not update:

- Case status,
- Appointment / Dispatch Visit status,
- Field Service Report status,
- `finalAppointmentId`,
- settlement approval,
- quote status,
- customer consent.

### Survey Context Should Use Final Completed Appointment

Future satisfaction survey should default to Case-level completion and final completed appointment context.

For multi-visit cases:

- survey context may reference completed report's stable `finalAppointmentId`,
- survey context may include safe visit history summary where policy allows,
- survey must not create multiple formal Field Service Reports,
- survey must not redefine which appointment is final.

## Data Separation Rules

### Survey Raw Response Must Stay Separate

Survey raw response, if future runtime exists, should be separate from:

- AI summary,
- AI risk flag,
- human review result,
- complaint record,
- callback record,
- supervisor note,
- Field Service Report internal note,
- audit log.

Raw survey response is customer-submitted data, not internal classification.

### AI Summary Must Stay Separate

AI summary may help reviewers read feedback faster.

It must not replace:

- the original customer feedback,
- human review outcome,
- complaint decision,
- callback result,
- official Field Service Report.

### Risk Flag Must Stay Separate

Risk flag is not official complaint.

Risk flag is not callback completion.

Risk flag is not quality review resolution.

Risk flag is not Case / Appointment / Field Service Report mutation.

### Human Review Result Must Be Distinct

Human review result should be recorded separately from AI suggestion and raw survey response in any future implementation.

It should identify:

- reviewer,
- review time,
- review outcome,
- next action,
- safe note category,
- whether callback / escalation / complaint / quality review remains open.

### Complaint And Callback Records Must Be Distinct

Complaint record and callback record are related but not identical.

Complaint may require callback, but callback does not always mean complaint.

Callback outcome does not automatically close complaint.

### Customer-visible Response Must Stay Safe

Customer-visible response must not expose:

- internal-only quality tag,
- supervisor note,
- AI raw payload,
- AI risk score,
- internal billing / settlement note,
- engineer internal comment,
- audit log,
- provider diagnostics,
- raw LINE user id,
- full customer mobile value,
- token,
- secret.

### Pricing / Fee Dispute Feedback Must Not Change Settlement Approval

Pricing / fee dispute feedback may create future Billing / Settlement review candidate.

It must not automatically:

- change customer consent,
- change quote status,
- change billing item,
- change settlement calculation,
- approve settlement adjustment,
- approve refund,
- modify invoice / payment state.

### Safety / Misconduct Wording Requires Human Priority

Safety concern, misconduct allegation, or serious service issue wording should be treated as high-priority human review candidate in future design.

AI may flag and summarize. AI must not determine truth, blame, discipline, compensation, or closure.

## Interaction With Existing Platform Objects

### Case

Survey feedback may reference Case context.

It must not automatically:

- reopen Case,
- close Case,
- change Case status,
- change Case completedAt,
- create duplicate Case.

### Appointment / Dispatch Visit

Survey feedback may reference final appointment or prior visits.

It must not automatically:

- reopen appointment,
- change appointment status,
- change visit result,
- create appointment,
- bypass one-open-appointment guard.

### Field Service Report

Survey feedback may reference completed report summary.

It must not automatically:

- change report status,
- change report completedAt,
- change report content,
- change `finalAppointmentId`,
- create another formal report.

### Survey Result

Survey result remains the original customer feedback source.

AI and human review should reference it, not overwrite it.

### Complaint / Callback Future Records

Complaint / callback records, if future implementation exists, should be created only by authorized human action or separately approved deterministic workflow.

Task269 does not approve those records.

### Billing / Settlement

Fee dispute feedback may become future Billing / Settlement review context.

It must not directly mutate billing, quote, customer consent, settlement, payment, or invoice state.

### Audit Log

Future audit should record important review actions, but audit logs remain internal-only and must use redacted summaries.

### AI Suggestion Records

AI suggestion records may reference survey feedback with safe metadata and source tracing.

They must not expose raw sensitive payloads or cross organization boundaries.

## SaaS-ready / Security Considerations

### Organization Isolation

Survey feedback, complaint candidates, callback candidates, quality review candidates, AI summaries, and AI risk flags must always be scoped by `organization_id`.

No mapping may cross tenant boundaries.

### Role / Permission Separation

Future implementation must decide which roles can:

- view survey feedback,
- view negative feedback classification,
- create complaint candidate,
- confirm formal complaint,
- assign callback,
- view callback history,
- view AI summary,
- accept / reject / edit AI suggestion,
- export quality reports.

Permission must remain distinct from organization entitlement.

### Customer-visible vs Internal-only Policy

Customer-submitted feedback is not the same as internal review classification.

Customer-visible surfaces must use safe content and must not reveal internal review labels, AI risk scores, supervisor notes, or internal billing / settlement data.

- customer mobile values,
