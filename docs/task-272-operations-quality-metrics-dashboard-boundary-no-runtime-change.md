# Task 272 - Operations / Quality Metrics and Dashboard Boundary / No Runtime Change

## Purpose And Scope

This document extends Task266 through Task271 with docs-only boundary guidance for future Operations / Quality metrics, dashboards, reports, exports, scheduled reports, and AI risk summaries.

It separates analytics and dashboard summaries from official Case, Appointment / Dispatch Visit, Field Service Report, complaint, callback, corrective action, engineer coaching, billing, settlement, and AI decision records.

## Why Metrics / Dashboard Boundaries Are Needed After Task271

Task271 separated corrective action and engineer coaching from official records.

Task272 defines how future aggregated metrics and dashboards should behave.

Metrics and dashboards can help supervisors and operators see risk, but they can also cause harm if they are treated as official decisions or exposed beyond permission boundaries.

The platform must avoid:

- treating dashboard summary as official Case record,
- using low-score metrics as automatic engineer discipline,
- treating complaint counts as complaint closure,
- exporting sensitive quality data without permission,
- showing internal supervisor notes in customer-facing dashboards,
- allowing scheduled reports to bypass data permissions,
- letting AI risk summary become supervisor decision.

Metrics should inform review. They should not mutate official records.

## Definitions

### Operations Quality Metric

Operations quality metric is an aggregated or counted signal about service quality, risk, feedback, callback, complaint, SLA, repeat visit, or coaching context.

It is not an official record by itself.

### Dashboard Summary

Dashboard summary is a visual or textual aggregation shown to an authorized role.

It is not official Case, Appointment, or Field Service Report data.

### Supervisor Quality View

Supervisor quality view is a future role-specific surface for reviewing quality risks, complaint candidates, callback status, engineer coaching candidates, and process improvement signals.

It must be permission-controlled and internal-only unless explicitly approved otherwise.

### Complaint Metric

Complaint metric counts or summarizes complaint candidates, confirmed complaints, complaint review outcomes, or escalation signals.

It is not complaint closure or complaint decision.

### Callback Metric

Callback metric counts or summarizes callback required candidates, callback attempts, callback outcomes, or unresolved follow-ups.

It is not callback workflow runtime.

### Survey Metric

Survey metric counts or summarizes survey response rate, score distribution, low-score rate, or feedback categories.

It is not survey runtime and not customer satisfaction truth by itself.

### Repeat Visit Metric

Repeat visit metric summarizes cases with multiple visits, repeat dissatisfaction, unresolved issue wording, or repeated incomplete outcomes.

It is review context, not automatic fault finding.

### Unresolved Case Metric

Unresolved case metric summarizes signals that may indicate a Case remains risky or unresolved.

It must not reopen or change Case status automatically.

### Engineer Coaching Metric

Engineer coaching metric summarizes internal coaching candidates or patterns.

It is not disciplinary record, payroll input, or performance penalty by default.

### SLA / Operations Risk Metric

SLA / operations risk metric summarizes overdue, stale, blocked, or high-risk operational signals.

It is not automatic escalation, notification sending, or Case mutation.

### AI Risk Summary

AI risk summary is an advisory summary generated by future AI.

It is not supervisor decision, official risk classification, complaint closure, or corrective action.

### Report / Export Candidate

Report / export candidate is a future data output that may be generated or downloaded by authorized users.

It must follow Data Access Control / Data Permission Model and must not bypass masking, permission, entitlement, audit, or usage tracking.

## Boundary Principles

### Dashboard Summary Is Not Official Record

Dashboard summary must not be treated as official Case, Appointment, Dispatch Visit, Field Service Report, complaint, callback, settlement, or coaching record.

Dashboards can point to underlying records. They must not replace them.

### Metrics Must Not Mutate Official Status

Metrics must not automatically update:

- Case status,
- Appointment status,
- visit result,
- Field Service Report status,
- `finalAppointmentId`,
- complaint status,
- callback status,
- settlement approval,
- payment / invoice state.

### Engineer Coaching Metrics Are Not Discipline

Engineer coaching metrics may identify review opportunities.

They must not automatically become:

- disciplinary record,
- negative performance score,
- payroll impact,
- settlement impact,
- customer-facing fault statement.

### Complaint / Callback Metrics Are Not Closure

Complaint and callback metrics summarize workflow state.

They must not close complaints, mark callbacks complete, or resolve escalation.

### AI Risk Summary Is Not Supervisor Decision

AI may summarize risk. AI cannot decide, close, approve, suppress, discipline, or mutate official records.

### Report / Export Must Not Bypass Data Access Control

Reports and exports must follow the same Data Access Control / Data Permission Model as interactive views.

Scheduled reports are automation, not a permission shortcut.

### Scheduled Reports Are Automation Layer Only

Scheduled reports, if future implementation exists, should reuse report / analytics / export rules.

They must not:

- expand data visibility,
- bypass field-level masking,
- send to unauthorized recipients,
- cross organization boundaries,
- expose internal-only data to customers.

## Data Access / Security Rules

### Unified Data Access Control Required

All future metrics, dashboards, reports, exports, and scheduled reports must use the same Data Access Control / Data Permission Model described in `docs/PROJECT_GUARDRAILS.md`.

At minimum, future implementation must check:

- organization scope,
- user identity,
- role,
- permission,
- report / export permission,
- feature entitlement,
- subscription status,
- allowed data scope,
- customer-visible policy,
- internal-only policy,
- field-level masking,
- audit requirement,
- usage tracking.

### Customer-visible Dashboard Must Be Safe

Customer-visible dashboard or portal, if future product adds it, must not expose:

- internal-only quality tag,
- supervisor note,
- engineer coaching note,
- AI raw payload,
- audit log,
- internal billing / settlement data,
- internal complaint classification,
- provider diagnostics,
- raw LINE user id,
- full customer mobile value,
- token,
- secret.

### Engineer-level Metrics Require Extra Care

Engineer-level metrics can be useful for supervisor review, but they are sensitive.

Future design must consider:

- minimum permission,
- supervisor scope,
- aggregation thresholds,
- whether engineer can view own metrics,
- whether metrics are coaching-only or performance-related,
- preventing unreviewed signals from becoming disciplinary conclusions.

### Small-sample Aggregation Needs Thresholds

Small-sample aggregation can reveal individual customers, engineers, or cases.

Future design should consider:

- minimum sample threshold,
- masking small groups,
- grouping by larger period,
- suppressing export for small groups,
- role-based access for sensitive drill-down,
- audit on exports.

## Interaction With Existing Platform Objects

### Case

Metrics may aggregate Case attributes or reference Case state.

They must not automatically:

- reopen Case,
- close Case,
- change Case status,
- change Case completedAt,
- create duplicate Case.

### Appointment / Dispatch Visit

Metrics may aggregate appointment outcomes.

They must not automatically:

- reopen appointment,
- create appointment,
- cancel appointment,
- complete appointment,
- change visit result,
- bypass one-open-appointment guard.

### Field Service Report

Metrics may aggregate report completion or quality context.

They must not automatically:

- change report status,
- change report completedAt,
- change report content,
- change `finalAppointmentId`,
- create another formal report.

### Survey Result

Metrics may aggregate survey response rate, scores, and feedback themes.

They must keep raw response, AI summary, internal classification, and human review separate.

### Complaint / Callback Future Records

Metrics may aggregate complaint and callback future records if implemented later.

They must not replace complaint / callback workflow decisions.

### Quality Review Future Records

Metrics may summarize quality review future records if implemented later.

They must not replace supervisor decision.

### Engineer Profile / Future Performance Context

Metrics may inform future performance context only after explicit policy.

Task272 does not approve engineer performance scoring.

### Billing / Settlement

Metrics may reference pricing / fee dispute counts.

They must not mutate billing, quote, customer consent, settlement, payment, or invoice state.

### Audit Log

Future report generation, dashboard access, export, scheduled report generation, and scheduled report delivery should be audit-ready.

Audit logs remain internal-only.

### AI Suggestion Records

Metrics may aggregate AI risk flags or AI suggestion usage.

AI metrics must not become official decisions.

### Report / Export Future Layer

Report / export layer must apply:

- organization isolation,
- role permission,
- report / export permission,
- field-level masking,
- customer-visible / internal-only policy,
- audit,
- usage tracking.

## SaaS-ready Considerations

### Organization Isolation

Metrics and dashboards must always be scoped by `organization_id`.

No dashboard, report, export, or scheduled report may cross tenant boundaries.

### Role / Permission Separation

Future implementation must decide which roles can:

- view operations quality dashboard,
- view supervisor quality dashboard,
- view engineer-level metrics,
- view complaint metrics,
- view callback metrics,
- view AI risk summary,
- export quality reports,
- configure scheduled reports,
- receive scheduled reports.

Permission must remain distinct from organization entitlement.

### Report / Export Permission Separation

Viewing a dashboard does not automatically grant export permission.

Exporting, downloading, or scheduled delivery may require separate permission and audit.

### Plan Entitlement

Possible future entitlements:

- `quality_dashboard`,
- `operations_quality_metrics`,
- `supervisor_quality_view`,
- `quality_report_export`,
- `scheduled_quality_report`,
- `engineer_coaching_metrics`,
- `ai_quality_risk_summary`,
- `ai_risk_radar`.

These are future planning examples only.

### Usage Tracking For Dashboard / Report / Export

Future usage tracking may apply to:

- dashboard views,
- report generation,
- exports,
- scheduled report generation,
- scheduled report delivery,
- AI summary generation,
- AI risk flag aggregation.

Usage tracking must not store unnecessary sensitive payloads.

### AI Add-on Usage Tracking

AI risk summary and AI dashboard explanation should be tracked separately from non-AI dashboard metrics.

AI Add-on does not bypass permission, organization scope, masking, audit, or customer-visible policy.

### Enterprise SSO Future Design

Enterprise SSO, if future product adds it, must not bypass:

- organization isolation,
- role / permission checks,
- report / export permission,
- audit log,
- data masking,
- entitlement checks.
